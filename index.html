Exercise 1A Problem 1

Problem Suppose aand bare real numbers, not both 0. Find real numbers cand dsuch that $$\frac{1}{a + bi} = c + di$$ Solution A standard trick when dealing with the reciprocal of a complex numbers is to multiply the numerator and the denominator by it’s complex conjugate, as this will make the denominator real. Applying this trick: \begin{equation} c + di = \frac{1}{a + bi} = \frac{a - bi}{a - bi} \cdot \frac{1}{a + bi} = \frac{a}{a^2 + b^2} + \frac{b}{a^2 + b^2}i \end{equation} From comparing the real and imaginary parts of the left and right hand sides above: \begin{align} &c = \frac{a}{a^2 + b^2} &&\land &&d=\frac{b}{a^2 + b^2} \end{align}
