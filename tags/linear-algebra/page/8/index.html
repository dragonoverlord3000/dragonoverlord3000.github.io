Problem Is the operation of addition on the subspaces of $V$ commutative? In other words, if $U$ and $W$ are subspaces of $V$, is $U + W = W + U$ ? Solution Yes, proof: $$U + W \stackrel{D1.36}{=} \lbrace u + w | u \in U, w \in W \rbrace = \stackrel{D1.19}{=} \lbrace w + u | w \in W, u \in U \rbrace \stackrel{D1.36}{=} W + U$$

Exercise 1C Problem 15

Problem Suppose $U$ is a subspace of $V$. What is $U + U$? Solution By directly applying the definition of sums of subspaces: $$U + U \stackrel{D1.36}{=} \lbrace 2u | u \in U \rbrace$$ Now let $v = 2u$, then $v \in U$, because $U$ is a subspace and thus closed under multiplication. Therefore: $$U + U = \lbrace v | v \in U \rbrace = U$$
